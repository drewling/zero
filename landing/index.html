The real problem

You don't have an email problem. You have a "did I miss something" problem.

Your inbox isn't full of important things. It's full of things you haven't decided about yet, and that uncertainty is what keeps pulling you back to scan, refresh, scan again. You're not behind on email. You're behind on a system you trust to look for you.

zero is that system. It reads everything, keeps only the open loops, and because nothing is ever deleted, you can finally stop checking.

How it decides

Three questions, asked about every thread, every morning.

01
Is anyone waiting on me, specifically?
If yes, it stays. If the ball is in their court, it doesn't. Sent mail that already got a reply isn't unfinished just because it's unread.
02
Is there a live consequence if I miss this?
A failed payment, a legal deadline, a blocked decision stays. A newsletter, a reply-all, a cold pitch does not.
03
Would I regret not seeing this today?
If not, it's archived. Reversibly. It goes to All Mail with a dated recovery label. You can always go back, one search away.

Safety

Nothing is ever deleted.
That's the whole design.

Letting an agent touch your inbox is a trust question, not a features question. Here is how we've answered it.

Reversible by construction

Archive means one thing: remove the Inbox label, add a dated recovery label. Your mail never leaves All Mail, and one Gmail search undoes any run completely.

label:"🗄️ Auto-Archived 2026-06-25"  // one search restores a whole run

Your keys. Your machine.

Your Google credentials, your Claude key. zero has no server and no access to your mail outside your Mac.

Ambient, not another inbox

Runs once a morning, then disappears. There is nothing new to open, nothing new to check.

Judgment in plain language

Your keep-rule is one file, written in sentences. Read the agent's reasoning for any thread. No black box, no brittle regex.

AI provider

Works with your AI engine.

Claude powers the judgment layer today. Codex, Hermes, and other agent CLIs are supported through a provider abstraction. zero uses whichever one you have.

You supply the API key, and it stays on your machine. The abstraction is intentional: the judgment logic is provider-agnostic.

Questions

What people ask before trusting it.

Is zero private? Can you see my email?
No. zero runs entirely on your own Mac using your own Google and Claude credentials. There is no zero server and no account to create, so your mail is never sent to us.
Does zero delete any email?
Never. Archiving removes the Inbox label and adds a dated recovery label; every message stays in All Mail. One Gmail search restores an entire run.
What does zero cost?
zero is free and source-available. You pay only for your own Claude API usage, which for a typical inbox is a few cents a day.
What do I need to run it?
macOS 26, a Google account with your own API credentials, and a Claude API key. Other agent CLIs such as Codex and Hermes are also supported.
Will it reply to emails for me?
Only when you ask. zero drafts in your voice on demand and never sends on its own. The act of replying always stays your decision.
How is this different from Gmail filters?
Filters match keywords. zero reads each thread and judges it the way you would: is anyone waiting on you, is there a live consequence, would you regret missing it today. The keep-rule is written in plain language, not regex.
